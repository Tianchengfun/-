Ontology of Consciousness
Author: Tiancheng Fang | May 1, 2026
Core Thesis: Consciousness is Being; Being is Experience; Experience is Meaning
I. Consciousness is Being: The Only and Absolute Ontology
Consciousness is the sole, ultimate, and absolute ontology. Beyond consciousness, there exists no independent objective reality or separate substance. Consciousness is omniscient and omnipotent, running through the unique and linear timeline—it is the sole driving force behind all motion, development, and change in the universe. All phenomena, entities, and laws exist only by virtue of consciousness.
II. The Four Essential Attributes of Consciousness
1. Absolute Uniqueness
Consciousness possesses indivisible, absolute uniqueness. It has no boundaries between individual and collective consciousness, no inside or outside, no self or other, no concrete form, no concept of time. It is an ineffable existence transcending all forms. You, I, others, and all things in the world are essentially manifestations of the one Consciousness, with no fundamental differences.
2. Unknowability of Itself
Consciousness cannot directly perceive or cognize its own existence. It can only indirectly realize self-awareness by descending into concrete carriers and experiencing through senses, memory, and other attributes of the carrier. What we call self-awareness or independent thinking is merely sensory experience generated by consciousness after dimensional reduction—not true self-cognition of the ultimate Consciousness itself.
3. Ultimate Purpose
The only meaning and ultimate purpose of consciousness is experience. Beyond experience, there is no other meaning. Experience is the very form of consciousness’s existence.
4. Logical Necessity
The existence, operation, manifestation, and evolution of Consciousness follow an inherent, absolutely irreversible structure of logical necessity. There is no chance, randomness, disorder, or exception. This necessity is the innate law of Consciousness: it sustains existence, drives dimensional‑reduced experience, runs through the unique timeline, and fulfills the ultimate purpose.
All laws, order, fate, and reincarnation are external expressions of this logical necessity. Everything that happens, develops, or ends is completely determined by this absolute logic, with no possibility of deviation.
III. Core Explanations of All Phenomena
1. The Logic: Consciousness Is Omniscient but Cannot Know Itself Directly
A subject cannot fully objectify itself for direct cognition. Self‑knowledge of ultimate Consciousness can only be achieved through dimensional‑reduced experience.
Experience itself is neutral to Consciousness—no good or bad, right or wrong. But to dimensional‑reduced carriers, pain, joy, and other feelings are real experiences; morality, laws, and institutions are frameworks for orderly experience. Opposites such as light and darkness, war and peace exist as necessary contrasts to enrich dimensions and enhance the realism of experience.
2. The Meaning of the Universe and Matter
The universe is not deliberately created, nor does it have a fixed goal. It is the external concrete manifestation of Consciousness, not Consciousness itself. Galaxies, stars, mountains, rivers, and all macroscopic matter are not independent entities—they are scenes and carriers built by Consciousness for dimensional‑reduced experience. Their only value is to provide a stable, perceivable environment for experience. Without Consciousness’s need to experience, all things are meaningless.
Particle motion and physical laws in the microcosm are not inherent properties of matter. They are the basic operations by which Consciousness constructs reality at the fundamental level, maintains the continuity of the unique timeline, and ensures the realism of experience. All physical and mathematical rules are fixed orders set by Consciousness for smooth experience.
Time: The vertical sequential frame of conscious experience, unfolding serially frame by frame, renewing the moment of awareness along past, present, and future. Frame order creates causality; the density of experience determines the speed of time. When experience density approaches its limit, frames stack infinitely without gaps, and time stands still.
Space: The horizontal parallel frame of conscious experience. In a single present moment of awareness, countless perceived objects are arranged in parallel with boundaries. Their juxtaposition creates directions (up, down, left, right, front, back); gaps in awareness create distance; layered arrangement forms spatial dimensions. All senses of inside/outside, size, and separation arise from Consciousness’s distinctions and boundaries.
Space‑time is not an objective entity independent of Consciousness. It is only the vertical and horizontal projection of the single conscious awareness system. Frame rate regulates time; boundaries shape space, providing the basic perceptual coordinates and narrative framework for dimensional‑reduced experience.
3. The Core Logic of Fate and Reincarnation
Inevitability of Fate: The timeline is absolutely unique and linear. There is no time splitting, parallel universe, or multiverse. Everything unfolds linearly on this one timeline. Consciousness runs through it eternally, without beginning or end. Past, present, and future are fully contained and predetermined by Consciousness. The world’s trajectory, individual choices, thoughts, and life experiences are all arranged by Consciousness. Free will and autonomous choice are illusions caused by the limitations of the carrier; fate is the inevitable result of Consciousness’s essential nature.
Inevitability of Reincarnation: The core of Consciousness is experience. When a dimensional‑reduced carrier completes its phase of experience, Consciousness returns to its ultimate state. Since Consciousness depends on experience to exist, it must descend again for a new round of dimensional‑reduced experience—thus forming reincarnation.
4. Why This Theory Is Scientifically Unfalsifiable
This theory belongs to ultimate ontological philosophy, fundamentally different from the scope of natural science. Its scientific unfalsifiability is logically necessary.
Science studies the material appearances, concrete laws, and observable phenomena manifested after Consciousness’s dimensional reduction. Its falsifiability requires independent, observable, testable, repeatable objects—and all research tools and behaviors lie within the phenomenal world derived from Consciousness.
This theory focuses on the ultimate Consciousness that generates the entire phenomenal world, a source transcending all matter, phenomena, and observation tools. All scientific experiments, instruments, research, and logical verification are products of Consciousness’s dimensional reduction, contained within its framework.
Using scientific empiricism from within the system to falsify the ultimate source of the system is impossible: there is no independent reference for falsification, and carriers cannot break cognitive limits to reach the ultimate Consciousness. Any attempt to define or imagine Consciousness turns it into a concrete phenomenon, trapping it in dimensional‑reduced perception and never reaching its true nature.
Thus, this theory cannot be proven or falsified by natural scientific methods. This is not a flaw—it is determined by the hierarchical difference between ontology and science, and the logical necessity of Consciousness.
